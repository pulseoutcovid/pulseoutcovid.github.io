Our Mission

Inspired by the New York Times Op-Ed by Dr. Richart Levitan, we seek to build low-cost, easy-to-use, open-source pulse oximeters to give to persons with active SARS-COVID-19 infection. It's been stated that individuals with the virus don't exhitbit normal signs of hypoxia, or low blood oxygen, until their blood oxygen levels are dangerously low. We believe that if every person diagnosed with the virus is given an easy-to-use pulse oximeter, sick individuals can seek medical attention before their blood oxygen becomes disasterously low.

The goal of this project is to build devices that are similar to a thermometer; they are another tool to help doctors in treating coronavirus patients. Our pulse oximeters will not diagnose the coronavirus. We're presently in the early prototyping phase of the project. After designing the first version of the device, we intend to partner with clinicians and manufacturers to validate, build, and distribute our designs.

We're a team of engineers who met in college while attending The Pennsylvania State University. This pandemic impacts all of us - our elderly and immunocompromised friends and family are all at risk. We're contibuting our time pro-bono to help fight this virus. If we can help at save at least one life, this project will be successful.
